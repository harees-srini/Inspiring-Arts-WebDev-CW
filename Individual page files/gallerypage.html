Ancient Portraits from Master Artists


Although portrait paintings, even self portraits have existed as long as art has, the renaissance era saw a revival in them because many rich families commisioned artists to paint pictures of themselves so that they could exist forever and to beautify their houses among other reasons. And since many artists were looking for money they did this no matter what. Take a look a some of these paintings.

Girl with a pearl earring

Selected as the most beautiful painting in the Netherlands, the Girl with the Pearl was drawn by Johannes Vermeer of in 1665 during the Dutch Golden Age. The painting is a tronie-meaning that it was more of a study of facial expression rather than a portrait. Although the highlight of the painting is the Pearl Earring, Vermeer use of light in the Girl's face and her moist lips display the richness of the Dutch Golden Age.

Mona Lisa

Calling it the most recognized portrait in the world would be understating the fame of the celebrated Mona Lisa. Drawn by the multi-talented artist Leonardo da Vinci, the portrait has the most mysterious backstory of any Renaissance paintings. Studies show that it was Da Vinci's most prized painting as well because he used to carry it with him wherever he went. Whatever it is, the sfumato style of this portrait still takes away the breath of those who look at it in awe.

Portrait of Dora Maar

Dora Markovic, a young Yugoslavic photographer who met Pablo Picasso found a place in one of his portraits. Picasso and Dora had become thick friends. The portrait is contains the usual features of Picasso's paintings. Highly vivid, different coloured eyes, face shown both in frontal and profile view. Even then, some say that the resemblance of this portrit is uncanny-even being 'truer than life'. Picasso has used those unique and vivid colours to provide depth and meaning to his portraits.

Celestina

Drawn by Pablo Picasso, this art was inspired by Picasso's own life as an unrecognized artist as well as his depression due to his friend's death. This is a painting of the blue period where the blueness of the painting was used to convey feelings of loss and misery. The woman here is partially blind and is partially based on a character of the same name from a Spanish play called Aurora Roja. This picture is strikingly representative of Picasso's belief that eyes were the most inmportant organ.

Self Portrait with Thorn Necklace and Hummingbird

This extremely unrealistic self portrait of Frida Kahlo drawn in the 1940s is in no way less of strong symbolism. This was drawn after her husband divorced her and her affair with a photographer ended. Frida's accident made her unable to bear children and left her bedridden for the most of her life. So the black monkey, cat and bird symbolize her loss of freedom. The thorn necklace is also a symbol as well. It should be known that many of Frida's portraits were self portraits and all of them contain tons of symbolism.

The Ugly Duchess

Painted by Quinten Massys in 1513 is actually known as the Old Woman and there was a huge mystery surrounding the art as to whether the woman actually looked like this. Da Vinci has had a long history of painting grotesques-pictures of abnormal faces created simply out of imagination. But recent researches have uncovered the fact that this portrait is that of a real woman's who had an exceptionally rare form of Paget's Disease-an abnormality that enlarges and deforms bones.

Woman with a Hat

Henri Matisse drew his wife as an oil on canvas in this painting. It was painted in 1905 and exhibited at the Automne Salon. Woman with a Hat drew a lot of controversy because of its unique brush strokes, vivid and unnatural colours and basically an unfinished look. This was also the painting that led to a style that came to be known as Fauvism- an extremely expressive style of painting. Although this painting was unfavourable among critics when it was exhibited, it became a huge center of attention among the general public.

Portrait of Dante

This unique portrait displays not just one but two of the most unique personalities of the Renaissance era. Sandro Botticelli and Dante Alighieri. Dante is known for his famous Inferno-a serious of poems about a man's journey from purgatory to hell to heaven. This portrait was drawn on sheepskin by Botticelli who was an ardent in poetic literature. He had loved Dante's poem so much in fact he had planned to draw each stanza of Dante's poem.
